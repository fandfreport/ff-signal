Scoring Framework · Proprietary

F&F Core
Framework

Five dimensions used to identify, evaluate, and rank early-stage companies shaping both the digital and physical systems of the future.

⬛ Proprietary to Friends & Family · All rights reserved
Five scoring dimensions
01
Innovation Score
How novel and category-defining the product or technology is — from incremental improvement to a breakthrough that creates an entirely new market.
1
Incremental
Minor improvement on existing solutions
2
Differentiated
Somewhat differentiated from competitors
3
Clearly novel
Clearly differentiated approach or model
4
Highly innovative
Significant technical or market innovation
5
Breakthrough
Category-defining; creates a new paradigm
02
Community Advancement Score
How much the company improves access, opportunity, or quality of life — from negligible community benefit to transformational effects on equity and livelihoods.
1
Minimal
Little to no direct community benefit
2
Indirect
Indirect benefit through economic activity
3
Moderate
Meaningful improvement to access or efficiency
4
Strong
Strong positive workforce or community effect
5
Transformational
Transformational impact on access, equity, or livelihoods
03
Water Score
Impact on water systems — spanning access, conservation, infrastructure, and resilience. A core F&F lens reflecting the belief that water is the defining infrastructure challenge of the next era.
1
None
No meaningful connection to water systems
2
Weak
Weak or indirect environmental relevance
3
Adjacent
Some sustainability or resource-efficiency relevance
4
Contributing
Clear contribution to water-adjacent resilience
5
Direct impact
Direct water infrastructure, access, or conservation impact
04
Market Visibility
How widely known or discussed the company is today — used to identify hidden innovators operating below the radar of mainstream VC and tech media attention.
1
Unknown
Almost entirely unknown outside founder networks
2
Niche aware
Early awareness in a specific niche community
3
Growing
Growing startup-ecosystem attention
4
Widely known
Widely discussed in VC and tech media
5
Mainstream
Highly visible; mainstream coverage and awareness
05
Civil Infrastructure Score
How much the business depends on or contributes to physical infrastructure systems — distinguishing pure software plays from companies deeply embedded in the built world.
1
Fully digital
Almost entirely software; no physical dependency
2
Light physical
Light physical dependency or distribution
3
Moderate
Moderate logistics, facility, or public-system reliance
4
Heavy reliance
Heavy reliance on transport, utilities, robotics, or physical networks
5
Deeply tied
Core business tied to energy, mobility, manufacturing, or public systems
